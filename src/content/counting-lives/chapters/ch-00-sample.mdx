## Introduction

Sample chapter introduction paragraph for layout testing. This section exercises
the full prose rendering pipeline: heading hierarchy, body text, sidenote grid
placement, and the sticky table of contents sidebar.

The mathematical foundation for this work draws on <ConceptTooltip client:visible concept="persistent homology" interlude="persistent-homology" definition="A method for measuring topological features of data — connected components, loops, and voids — that persist across multiple scales of resolution." /> to reveal structural patterns in poverty data that conventional statistics obscure.<sup aria-describedby="sn-1">1</sup>

<Sidenote number={1}>Persistent homology was developed by Edelsbrunner, Letscher and Zomorodian (2002) and has since become a cornerstone of topological data analysis.</Sidenote>

### First Section

The notion of a <ConceptTooltip client:visible concept="Betti number" interlude="betti-numbers" definition="A sequence of integers counting the number of independent holes in each dimension of a topological space: β₀ counts components, β₁ counts loops, β₂ counts voids." /> provides a compact algebraic summary of topological complexity.<sup aria-describedby="sn-2">2</sup>

<Sidenote number={2}>Betti numbers are named after the Italian mathematician Enrico Betti (1871), though their modern formulation is due to Poincaré.</Sidenote>
