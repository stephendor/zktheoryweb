## Dataset Description

The British Household Panel Survey (BHPS) ran from 1991 to 2009, yielding 18 annual waves of data from an initial sample of approximately 10,000 households across Great Britain. The BHPS was the predecessor to Understanding Society (UKHLS) and was designed for continuity with it; the University of Essex constructed a linked panel bridging both surveys, enabling trajectory analysis spanning 1991 to the present.

For this research programme, the BHPS provides the historical extension of employment trajectories — particularly the period covering the early 1990s recession (1991–1995), the long expansion of the late 1990s, and the 2000s pre-crisis period. These historical periods are essential for the zigzag persistence analysis in Paper 3 and for the intergenerational matching analysis in Paper 6, which requires parent trajectories observed from the 1990s.

Monthly activity sequences are constructed from the annual activity calendar variable, following the coding scheme harmonised with Understanding Society. Annual data are supplemented with retrospective employment history questions for periods before 1991 where available.

## Access Information

BHPS data are archived with the **UK Data Service (UKDS)** and are available under Safeguarded Licence to registered academic researchers. The data are subject to the same access and use conditions as Understanding Society.

**UKDS SN:** 5151 (all waves combined)  
**Access portal:** [UK Data Service](https://ukdataservice.ac.uk/)

The linked BHPS–UKHLS panel (SN 7786) is used in this programme in preference to the standalone BHPS files wherever analysis spans both survey periods.

## Ethics and Consent

BHPS participants gave written informed consent at enrolment and at each annual re-interview. The BHPS received ethical approval from the University of Essex. As with Understanding Society, re-use under the UKDS Safeguarded Licence does not require separate ethical review, but all analysis conditions apply — including non-disclosure of individual-level data and de-identification requirements.

The intergenerational analysis in Paper 6 links parent and offspring records using household identifiers present in the licensed data files. This linkage is explicitly permitted under the UKDS licence terms for analytical household-level studies and does not constitute additional data collection or further personal data processing.
