## Dataset Description

Understanding Society (UKHLS) is the UK's primary general population longitudinal household panel survey. It follows approximately 40,000 households across the UK, collecting annual interviews from all adult members. The survey began in 2009 as the successor to the British Household Panel Survey (BHPS) and is designed for continuity with the BHPS so that a linked 1991–present panel can be constructed.

For this research programme, Understanding Society provides the primary source of monthly activity sequences, used to construct employment trajectories covering up to 14 annual waves (2009–2022). The employment state coding — employed, self-employed, unemployed, inactive, in education, retired — follows the harmonised scheme developed for the BHPS–UKHLS linkage.

Key variables used: monthly activity calendar, annual earnings, equivalised household income, highest educational qualification, occupation (SOC 2010), industry sector (SIC 2007), household composition, and neighbourhood identifiers (NUTS-3 look-up).

## Access Information

Understanding Society data are deposited with the **UK Data Service (UKDS)** and are available under an End User Licence (EUL) to registered UK academic researchers, and to international researchers via Safeguarded Licence. Registration requires institutional affiliation and agreement to terms of use.

## Ethics and Consent

Understanding Society study participants gave written informed consent at enrolment and at each re-interview. The study has continuous ethical approval from the University of Essex Ethics Committee. Re-use of archived data under the End User Licence does not require independent ethical approval, but researchers are required to use the data consistent with the original consent — including that findings must not identify individual participants.

All analyses in this programme use person-level data at a minimum cell size ensuring no individual is identifiable. No sensitive characteristics are linked beyond those provided in the licensed data files.
