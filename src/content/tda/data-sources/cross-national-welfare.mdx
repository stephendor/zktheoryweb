## Dataset Description

The European Union Statistics on Income and Living Conditions (EU-SILC) is a harmonised longitudinal microdata infrastructure collecting employment, income, poverty, and living conditions data across EU member states and several associated countries. The longitudinal component follows individual respondents across up to four annual waves, enabling short-panel trajectory construction.

For this research programme, EU-SILC provides the cross-national comparator data for **Paper 5** (welfare state topology comparison), the transfer learning validation dataset for **Paper 9** (CCNN cross-national transferability), and the cross-national fairness robustness assessment in **Paper 10**. The programme uses EU-SILC data for 16 European countries spanning 2004–2020, harmonised to a common employment state coding comparable to the BHPS/UKHLS scheme.

Key variables: annual labour status, monthly activity calendar (where available), equivalised household income, highest educational attainment, industry sector, household composition, and country × year identifiers enabling welfare state regime classification.

The EU-SILC panel structure (4-year rotating panels) means individual trajectories are shorter than BHPS/UKHLS trajectories; analysis is restricted to the within-panel window and supplemented by cross-sectional repeated samples where longer spans are needed.

## Access Information

EU-SILC microdata are held by **Eurostat** and released to researchers under a confidentiality agreement via the **Eurostat Safe Centre** (on-site) or, for approved projects, via a remote access facility.

**Eurostat reference:** EU-SILC UDB (User Database, longitudinal component)  
**Application portal:** [Eurostat microdata access](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/web/microdata)

Applications require institutional affiliation, a research proposal outlining the scientific case for access, and agreement to the Eurostat microdata confidentiality rules. Access is typically granted for a fixed period tied to the approved project.

## Ethics and Consent

EU-SILC is conducted under the authority of Regulation (EC) No 1177/2003. Member state National Statistical Institutes are responsible for participant recruitment, informed consent, and ethical compliance under national legislative frameworks. Re-use of archived Eurostat microdata under the standard access agreement requires compliance with Eurostat confidentiality obligations but does not require a separate research ethics application from the secondary analyst.

No individual-level data are retained beyond the approved analysis period. All outputs are subject to the Eurostat statistical disclosure control rules before publication.
