# P02 Argument Structure

## Core Claim

Mapper applied to the PCA-20D trajectory embedding reveals within-regime heterogeneity — sub-regions of the trajectory space with distinct escape probabilities — that the 7-GMM typology and global PH both compress away. The Mapper graph provides a navigable "map" of trajectory space where outcomes are geographically readable.

## Planned Structure

1. **Introduction** — limitation of global topology (P01) and sequence typologies for interior structure; Mapper as resolution
2. **Literature** — Mapper (Singh, Mémoli, Carlsson 2007), KeplerMapper; within-regime heterogeneity in life-course research
3. **Data & Methods** — reuse P01 embedding; Mapper parameter choices (projection, cover, clustering); node-colouring by outcome variables
4. **Results**
   - Mapper graph topology: node count, edge structure, giant component vs. peripheral nodes
   - Regime validation: do Mapper nodes reproduce GMM regime labels? (ARI check)
   - Within-regime heterogeneity: sub-regions of Low-Income Churn and Mixed Churn with different escape rates
   - Causal geography: income endpoint, NS-SEC, cohort mapped onto graph nodes
5. **Discussion** — Mapper as complement to global PH; comparison with OM; within-regime heterogeneity implications for intervention design
6. **Conclusion**
