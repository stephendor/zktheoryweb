\begin{abstract}
Mapper \citep{singh2007topological}, applied to 27,280 employment-income
trajectories from Understanding Society (2009--2023) in PCA-20D space,
reproduces the seven Gaussian mixture model (GMM) regimes at node level
(purity~0.999, NMI~=~0.434) while fragmenting each regime into sub-regions
that vary on the PC1 income gradient. Low-Income Churn splits into 80 nodes
spanning a 4-unit PC1 range (node-mean std~=~1.23); 32 of these deviate by
more than one standard deviation from the regime mean. Across all seven
regimes, 358 of 1,060 nodes qualify as sub-regime outliers; permutation null
tests confirm this exceeds chance ($\pval < 0.01$, both regime-shuffle and
within-node-shuffle nulls). A sensitivity analysis across 24
configurations --- two clustering algorithms, four lens functions, three
parameter values each --- shows that the 49\% noise rate and the absence of
bridge nodes are DBSCAN properties, not data properties: agglomerative
clustering covers 100\% of trajectories and identifies 203--449 bridge nodes
spanning regime boundaries. Within-regime heterogeneity persists across all 24
configurations within PCA-20D and replicates qualitatively under UMAP-16D,
though specific node counts are embedding-dependent. Colouring nodes by
reconstructed trajectory outcomes reveals a within-R6 bifurcation: nodes near
the Employed Mid boundary show employment rates of~0.7 and final income
trending mid-range; nodes near Inactive Low show rates below~0.3 and
persistently low income.

\medskip\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} Mapper algorithm, topological data analysis,
trajectory analysis, within-regime heterogeneity, employment trajectories,
KeplerMapper, sensitivity analysis, outcome geography
\end{abstract}
